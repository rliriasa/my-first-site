This is not a consensus operation. There is no coverage committee, no house view, no client to appease. There is one analyst with a genuine obsession with markets, a fleet of AI agents running research around the clock, and a mandate to find the most asymmetric longs on the planet.

The foundation is capital markets. Trained on a Wall Street trading desk — watching how institutional money actually moves, not in textbooks, but in real time, under pressure, with real capital at stake. That experience doesn't teach you what to buy. It teaches you how markets behave, how price discovery works, and why most investors are wrong at exactly the wrong moments.

The focus came from the classroom and never left. Emerging markets economics — the structural forces behind LatAm, India, the Gulf, Southeast Asia — studied formally and obsessed over privately. The kind of obsession where you are reading central bank minutes on a Saturday not because you have to, but because you genuinely cannot stop.

The AI layer is what makes the depth possible. Agents monitor filings, transcripts, and signals 24 hours a day across all ten names. I set the thesis. I make the calls. The agents make sure nothing slips through.

Research philosophy
"Find the best businesses in the world operating in the largest addressable markets, buy them when they are unfairly punished, and let compounding do the rest. Then use every tool available to find them faster than anyone else."

What this is
Pillar I — Emerging Markets
Dominant franchises in high-growth, underserved economies. LatAm, India, South Korea, Saudi Arabia, Argentina. Businesses compounding at rates impossible in developed markets, priced at discounts that reflect geography rather than quality.
Pillar II — Futurist Bets
Early-stage physical technology companies building infrastructure that will either dominate or disappear within a decade. Space launch, satellite connectivity, advanced nuclear, the lunar economy. Small positions. Large asymmetry.
