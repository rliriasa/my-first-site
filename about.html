INVESTMENTS
Est. 2025 · Targeting $1B AUM · Founder-Led

Liria Investments is not a traditional fund. There is no analyst team, no coverage committee, no consensus process. There is one founder with a genuine obsession with markets, a growing fleet of AI agents running research around the clock, and a relentless focus on two opportunity sets that most allocators systematically under-own.

The foundation is capital markets. Trained inside a Wall Street trading desk, watching how institutional money actually moves — not in textbooks, but in real time, under pressure, with real capital at stake. That experience doesn't teach you what to buy. It teaches you how markets behave, how price discovery works, and why most investors are wrong at exactly the wrong moments.

The focus came from the classroom and never left. Emerging markets economics — the structural forces behind LatAm, India, the Gulf, Southeast Asia — studied formally and obsessed over privately. The kind of obsession where you are reading central bank minutes on a Saturday not because you have to, but because you genuinely cannot stop.

The AI layer is what makes the structure possible. A single founder running a concentrated book with the research depth of a full team — because the agents never sleep, never miss an earnings call, and never get distracted. The target is $1 billion in assets under management, built through a high-conviction book that stays lean by design.

Investment Philosophy
"Find the best businesses in the world operating in the largest addressable markets, buy them when they are unfairly punished, and let compounding do the rest. Then use every tool available to find them faster than anyone else."

Capital markets background: trained on a Wall Street trading desk — institutional market structure, how large orders move prices, and how capital flows under real conditions that no model fully captures
Emerging markets focus: formal academic grounding in EM economics combined with years of independent research across LatAm, India, the Gulf, and South Korea — the conviction comes from both the theory and the obsession
AI-native from day one: the fund was built around agents, not retrofitted with them. Every research process was designed to be automated first, human-judged second — which is why one person can run this book with genuine depth

Always-on monitoring: agents track filings, transcripts, macro releases, short interest changes, and news across all coverage names 24 hours a day
Signal extraction: models parse earnings calls for tone shifts, capex guidance changes, and language patterns that tend to precede estimate revisions before consensus catches up
Model automation: financial models update automatically on new data releases, scenarios refresh in real time, sensitivity tables rebuild without manual intervention
Lean by design: the research output of a 6-person team, running on one founder and a growing agent fleet — the cost base stays minimal, the alignment stays clean

The mandate
Pillar I: Emerging Markets
Dominant franchises in high-growth, underserved economies. LatAm, India, South Korea, Saudi Arabia, Argentina. Businesses compounding at rates impossible in developed markets, priced at discounts that reflect geography rather than quality.
Pillar II: Futurist Bets
Early-stage physical technology companies building infrastructure that will either dominate or disappear within a decade. Space launch, satellite connectivity, advanced nuclear, the lunar economy. Small positions, large asymmetry, patient capital.
Target AUM
$1 billion. Enough to be a serious allocator at every name in the book. Small enough to stay nimble and concentrated. We have no interest in growing beyond that if it means diluting the edge.
Time Horizon
3 to 5 years minimum on core positions. We are not trading. We are buying businesses we want to own through cycles, with the patience that most institutions claim to have but rarely demonstrate.
Structure
One managing partner. No junior analysts. A suite of proprietary AI research agents. External prime brokerage and fund administration. Institutional-grade infrastructure at a fraction of the traditional cost base.
What we are not
A quant fund. A macro fund. A multi-strategy platform. A fund of funds. We do one thing: find the best asymmetric long opportunities on the planet in two defined categories, and own them with conviction.
