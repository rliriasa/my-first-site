Weight Optimization Logic
Weights are sized by risk-adjusted expected return — a combination of conviction in the thesis, liquidity, and downside asymmetry. Highest weights go to high-quality compounders (MELI, NU) where thesis optionality is intact even during drawdowns. Futurist bets are capped at 3–12% each, reflecting binary optionality rather than certainty.
EM Pillar · 70% Allocation
Anchored by three dominant digital-financial franchises in LatAm and South Asia: MELI (18%), NU (15%), IBN (12%). RJHI and SSNLF provide diversification across Saudi and Korean market cycles. GGAL is a high-beta Argentina macro bet kept small. Combined, these six names give exposure to ~4 billion underbanked consumers in the highest-growth economies on earth.
Futurist Pillar · 30% Allocation
ASTS and RKLB carry the largest weights (12% each) given their revenue visibility, backlog depth, and defensible network effects in emerging space infrastructure. OKLO and LUNR are smaller, higher-variance bets on nuclear micro-reactors and lunar logistics. All four are sized for everything-or-nothing outcomes over a 5–10 year horizon.
